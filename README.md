# Scope:
ROCK VR aims to port all poses from the Indie video game RUMBLE, by Buckethead Entertainment, as well as further integrating it into a Minecraft setting with features such as Stone Armor. Earthbending VR is not affiliated with Buckethead Entertainment, but I encourage players who enjoy this mod to check out the (much better) full game.

# Current Features:
<h2> Poses:</h2>
  The following poses are ways you can summon rocks and manipulate them. They all have advanced uses found via experimentation such as the Speed Disc (Applying Straight to a Disc to launch it faster) or even flight, but there are too many to list here.
<h3> Straight</h3>
  Pushes the closest stone forward, as if you had punched it really, really, hard.
<h3> Ball</h3>
  Launches a ball of rock from the floor in front of you. Strength: 1
<h3> Uppercut</h3>
  Pushes a rock upwards and forward, but to a lesser degree than Straight and Kick.
<h3> Kick</h3>
  Pushes a rock directly upwards and ungrounds it.

<h2> Miscellaneous: </h2>
<h3> Stone Armor: </h3>
  Stone Armor has a durability between iron and chainmail, the same defence as iron, and a higher knockback resistance than Netherite due to its weight. Once further features are added, wearing a full set of Stone Armor will allow the wearer to perform poses despite not being within the required proximity of stone.


# Planned Features:
<h2> Full VR Integration</h3>
  ROCK VR will use your VR controller positions to check if you're performing a pose.
<h2> Poses:</h2>
  The following poses are ways you can summon rocks and manipulate them. They all have advanced uses found via experimentation such as the Speed Disc (Applying Straight to a Disc to launch it faster) or even flight, but there are too many to list here.
<h3> Disc</h3>
  Launches a small moving disc projectile in the direction you're looking. Strength: 0
<h3> Pillar</h3>
  Erects a grounded pillar of rock in front of you. Strength: 1
<h3> Stomp</h3>
  Pushes a rock directly downwards until it reaches the floor, grounding it when it reaches the floor. It also stops all horizontal momentum.
<h3> Wall</h3>
  Erects a grounded wall of rock in front of you. Strength: 3
<h3> Jump</h3>
  Launches you approximately 2 blocks upward, maintaining momentum.
<h3> Cube</h3>
  Erects a grounded cube of rock in front of you. Strength: 2
<h3> Dash</h3>
  Pushes you directly in the horizontal direction you're looking with a high acceleration.
<h3> Parry</h3>
  Stops all momentum on a rock and ungrounds it.
<h3> Hold</h3>
  Allows you control the affected rock as if it were attached to your hand, from a small distance. An ungrounded rock can be held using both hands, giving more control and higher sensitivity in its movements, but a grounded rock can only be held by one, swapping hands if the pose is used again on it. You cannot control the rotation of rocks being held by rotating your hands. A hold will break if a structure is moving more than approximately 4 blocks per second.
<h3>Explode</h3>
  Permanently modifies a rock so when it breaks, all rocks within a radius are ungrounded and pushed away from the explosion proportionally to the strength of the exploded rock.
<h3> Flick</h3>
  Similar to hold, flick keeps a rock a small distance from your hands. You can only flick an ungrounded rock and flicked rocks are manipulated using both hands. By moving your hands, you can control the rotation of the flicked rock.
<h2> Shiftstones</h2>
  Shiftstones are equippable gemstones you attach to your hands to change the way your poses work. They will be elaborated upon further when they are added, but for now, you can check the RUMBLE Wiki (link at the bottom of this page) to see how they work in the source material.

# Mechanics:
Grounded = Attached to the floor, strength increased by 1.

Moving = Strength increased by 1.
